Collatz Conjecture

3(x) + 1

The Collatz Conjecture, also known as the 3(x) + 1 conjecture, is a famous unsolved problem in mathematics. It proposes a sequence defined by the following rules: Start with any positive integer x. If x is even, divide it by 2; if n is odd, multiply it by 3 and add 1. The conjecture posits that no matter the starting number, the sequence will eventually reach the value 1.

Goldbach Conjecture

Goldbach Conjecture

The Goldbach Conjecture is a famous unsolved problem in number theory that dates back to 1742. It was first proposed by the German mathematician Christian Goldbach, who wrote a letter to the famous mathematician Leonhard Euler in which he stated that "every positive even integer greater than 2 can be expressed as the sum of two prime numbers."

Twin Prime Conjecture

Twin Prime Conjecture

The twin prime conjecture is a famous unsolved problem in number theory that deals with the distribution of twin prime numbers. Twin primes are pairs of prime numbers that differ by 2, such as (3, 5), (5, 7), (11, 13), and so on. The twin prime conjecture states that there are an infinite number of twin prime pairs.

Fibonacci Sequence

Fn = Fn-1 + Fn-2

The Fibonacci sequence is a fundamental mathematical sequence where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones, starting from 0 and 1. So, the sequence begins 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, and so on. It has numerous applications in various fields, including mathematics, biology, art, and computer science and appears in natural phenomena, such as the arrangement of leaves on a stem or the spirals in a seashell.
